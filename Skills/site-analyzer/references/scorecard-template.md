# Launch Readiness Scorecard Template

Use this structure when the user asks for a scorecard, launch readiness assessment, or go/no-go evaluation. The Executive Summary is the longest section because it contains the decision and actions — this is intentional.

1. **Executive Summary & Verdict** — This is the MOST IMPORTANT section. Include ALL of the following in this one section:
   - Composite score (X/100) and one-sentence headline
   - **GO / NO-GO RECOMMENDATION** with confidence level (e.g., "NO-GO at 85% confidence") and 2-3 conditions that would change the verdict
   - **TOP 3 ACTIONS** — numbered list, each with: specific action, owner, deadline, and consequence if skipped. Example: "1. **Decide ZDTC audit strategy this week** (Sarah Chen) — every day of delay makes June 15 mathematically impossible. Trade-off: fast-track costs $50-80K from contingency vs. dropping certification risks losing enterprise buyers."

2. **Health Dimension Scores** — Table: Dimension | Score | Status | Key Signal. Maximum 8 rows. No prose outside table.

3. **Cross-Source Discrepancies** — Table: # | Discrepancy | Source A vs Source B | Severity. Maximum 5 rows — pick the most critical. No prose outside table.

4. **Risk Matrix** — Table: Risk | Owner | L×I Score | Mitigation | Flag. Maximum 6 rows. No prose outside table.

5. **Budget Health** — Table: Line | Allocated | Spent | % | Flag. Maximum 6 rows. No prose outside table.

6. **Critical Path** — Top 3-4 dependency chains. Use compact notation (A → B → C). One paragraph each, maximum.

**HARD RULES:**
- Tables ONLY — no explanatory prose between or after tables (except Critical Path which uses compact paragraphs)
- The Executive Summary & Verdict section MUST contain the go/no-go decision AND the top 3 actions. These are non-negotiable.
- If running long, cut rows from Budget and Risk Matrix first. NEVER cut the Executive Summary & Verdict.
